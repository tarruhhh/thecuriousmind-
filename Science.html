Essential Science Knowledge

Everything you should know about Science

1. Physics: The Science of Matter and Energy

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Describes how objects move in response to forces.
  • Einstein's Theory of Relativity: Explains the relationship between space, time, and gravity.
  • The Laws of Thermodynamics: Governs the principles of energy transfer and transformation.
  • Electromagnetism: Describes the interaction of electric and magnetic fields.

2. Chemistry: The Science of Substances

  • Atoms and Molecules: Everything is made of atoms, which bond to form molecules.
  • Periodic Table: Organizes all known elements based on atomic number and properties.
  • Chemical Reactions: The process where substances are transformed into new substances.
  • Acids and Bases: Acids release H+ ions, while bases release OH- ions in water.

3. Biology: The Science of Life

  • Cell Theory: All living organisms are made of cells, which are the basic units of life.
  • Evolution: Species change over time through the process of natural selection.
  • Genetics: The study of heredity and the variation of inherited characteristics.
  • Ecology: The study of how organisms interact with each other and their environment.

4. Earth Science: Understanding Our Planet

  • Plate Tectonics: The Earth's lithosphere is divided into tectonic plates that move over time.
  • Water Cycle: The continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth.
  • Climate Change: A long-term change in the Earth's climate due to human activities and natural factors.
  • Geological Time: A timescale used by geologists to describe the timing of events in Earth's history.

5. The Scientific Method: How We Discover Knowledge

  • Observation: Making detailed observations of the world around us.
  • Hypothesis: Formulating an educated guess or prediction based on observations.
  • Experiment: Testing the hypothesis through controlled experimentation.
  • Analysis: Analyzing data to draw conclusions and refine the hypothesis.
  • Conclusion: Final judgment based on experimental results, leading to further questions.
