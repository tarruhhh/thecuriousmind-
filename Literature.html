Literature

Learn about English writing and speaking

English & Literature Curriculum (Grade 1 - 12)

Grade 1 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Identifying and naming common objects, people, and places.
  • Recognizing simple sight words and high-frequency words.
  • Understanding basic story structure (beginning, middle, end).

Phonics and Phonemic Awareness

  • Recognizing letter sounds and blending them into words.
  • Identifying rhyming words and syllables in words.
  • Beginning to decode simple words.

Writing

  • Writing simple sentences with proper capitalization and punctuation.
  • Writing about personal experiences and familiar topics.
  • Using basic sight words and phonetic spelling.

Grammar

  • Recognizing and using capital letters and punctuation marks.
  • Identifying and using nouns, verbs, and adjectives in sentences.
  • Understanding singular and plural forms.

Literary Elements

  • Identifying characters, setting, and events in a story.
  • Understanding basic concepts of a story (plot, character, conflict).

Grade 2 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Understanding the main idea and supporting details of a story.
  • Sequencing events in a story (beginning, middle, end).
  • Answering simple questions about the text.

Phonics and Vocabulary

  • Expanding vocabulary using context clues.
  • Recognizing common word patterns and digraphs.
  • Identifying common prefixes and suffixes.

Writing

  • Writing short descriptive paragraphs.
  • Writing personal narratives and simple stories.
  • Using transition words like "first," "next," and "then."

Grammar

  • Using subject-verb agreement in simple sentences.
  • Identifying adjectives and adverbs.
  • Understanding singular and plural possessives.

Literary Elements

  • Identifying story characters, setting, and problem.
  • Recognizing the difference between fiction and nonfiction.
  • Understanding simple plots and themes.

Grade 3 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Summarizing key details and events from a text.
  • Identifying cause and effect relationships.
  • Understanding character motivation and basic themes.

Vocabulary Development

  • Using dictionaries and thesauruses to find word meanings.
  • Recognizing synonyms and antonyms.
  • Learning and using words related to different subjects.

Writing

  • Writing clear and organized paragraphs with a main idea and supporting details.
  • Writing letters and reports.
  • Revising and editing their own writing for clarity and detail.

Grammar

  • Using proper sentence structure, including compound sentences.
  • Correctly using commas, quotation marks, and apostrophes.
  • Using correct tense and verb forms.

Literary Elements

  • Understanding plot structure (exposition, rising action, climax, resolution).
  • Identifying themes, character traits, and setting.
  • Analyzing characters' actions and motivations.

Grade 4 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing story structure and identifying literary elements.
  • Understanding and interpreting the theme of a story.
  • Making predictions and inferences based on text evidence.

Vocabulary and Word Study

  • Expanding vocabulary through context and word parts (prefixes, suffixes).
  • Understanding figurative language, idioms, and metaphors.
  • Using context to determine the meaning of unfamiliar words.

Writing

  • Writing persuasive essays and narratives.
  • Organizing writing into clear paragraphs with main ideas and supporting details.
  • Using correct spelling, punctuation, and grammar in writing.

Grammar

  • Using complex sentences with conjunctions.
  • Understanding and applying subject-verb agreement in various tenses.
  • Proper use of adjectives, adverbs, and prepositions.

Literary Elements

  • Identifying and analyzing character development, conflict, and resolution.
  • Recognizing different types of poems and their structures.
  • Understanding point of view (first person, third person).

Grade 5 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing the plot, theme, and character development in novels and short stories.
  • Identifying the author's purpose and tone.
  • Comparing and contrasting characters, settings, and events.

Vocabulary Development

  • Using root words, prefixes, and suffixes to understand unfamiliar words.
  • Learning figurative language such as similes, metaphors, and personification.
  • Building academic vocabulary for various subjects.

Writing

  • Writing detailed essays and reports with a clear introduction, body, and conclusion.
  • Using transitions and connectors to improve writing flow.
  • Editing and revising writing for clarity and structure.

Grammar

  • Understanding verb tenses (past, present, future).
  • Using commas, semicolons, and colons correctly.
  • Writing complex sentences using conjunctions and relative pronouns.

Literary Elements

  • Understanding irony, foreshadowing, and flashbacks in stories.
  • Identifying different genres (fiction, nonfiction, drama, poetry).
  • Analyzing how authors use dialogue and description.

Grade 6 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing the main idea, plot, and theme in more complex texts.
  • Comparing and contrasting texts and characters.
  • Identifying the tone, mood, and author’s purpose.

Vocabulary Development

  • Expanding vocabulary using context and dictionaries.
  • Understanding and analyzing idiomatic expressions and figurative language.
  • Using academic and domain-specific vocabulary in writing and speaking.

Writing

  • Writing narratives, expository essays, and persuasive essays.
  • Incorporating quotes, evidence, and details to support arguments.
  • Organizing writing effectively and revising for clarity.

Grammar

  • Using various sentence structures (complex, compound, compound-complex).
  • Correct use of prepositions, conjunctions, and clauses.
  • Using proper punctuation, including quotation marks and commas in complex sentences.

Literary Elements

  • Analyzing symbolism, theme, and conflict.
  • Identifying narrative perspectives (first, second, and third person).
  • Understanding character motivation and development.

Grade 7 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing plot structure, character development, and theme in novels and short stories.
  • Identifying and analyzing the use of literary devices such as symbolism and foreshadowing.
  • Understanding multiple perspectives and themes in texts.

Vocabulary Development

  • Learning advanced vocabulary, idiomatic expressions, and figurative language (e.g., similes, metaphors).
  • Expanding vocabulary through root words, prefixes, and suffixes.
  • Using context clues and dictionaries to understand new words.

Writing

  • Writing analytical essays and creative stories.
  • Writing research papers with proper citations and referencing.
  • Using transitions and connectors to improve the structure of writing.

Grammar

  • Understanding complex sentence structures, including relative clauses and subordinate clauses.
  • Mastering punctuation rules, including semicolons, colons, and commas in complex sentences.
  • Understanding and using direct and indirect speech.

Literary Elements

  • Identifying themes, character traits, and conflict in literature.
  • Analyzing the impact of setting on the plot and characters.
  • Studying different literary genres (e.g., realism, fantasy, drama).

Grade 8 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Evaluating the use of literary devices (e.g., imagery, symbolism, irony) and their impact on the text.
  • Analyzing complex plots, conflicts, and character motivations in fiction and nonfiction.
  • Comparing and contrasting characters, settings, and themes across different texts.

Vocabulary Development

  • Expanding vocabulary through reading and identifying new words in context.
  • Learning specialized vocabulary related to different genres (e.g., technical, literary, historical terms).
  • Using context clues and root words to derive meanings of unfamiliar words.

Writing

  • Writing critical response essays, comparing and contrasting different texts or characters.
  • Organizing research papers, reports, and formal letters.
  • Improving writing clarity and coherence through effective paragraphing and transitions.

Grammar

  • Understanding and applying various sentence types (compound, complex, and compound-complex).
  • Using punctuation correctly in complex sentences (colons, semicolons, dashes).
  • Mastering the use of conditionals and modals in both writing and speech.

Literary Elements

  • Exploring narrative perspectives and point of view in novels and plays.
  • Identifying symbols, motifs, and allegories in texts.
  • Understanding the role of theme, setting, and character in influencing the overall meaning of a text.

Grade 9 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing and interpreting advanced literary texts, identifying themes, symbols, and motifs.
  • Identifying the author's tone, purpose, and style in various types of texts.
  • Evaluating the effectiveness of literary devices (e.g., symbolism, irony, metaphor) in creating meaning.

Vocabulary Development

  • Expanding vocabulary using sophisticated context clues and derivational morphemes (prefixes, suffixes).
  • Building academic and subject-specific vocabulary to express complex ideas and arguments.
  • Learning and using advanced literary terms (e.g., anaphora, allusion, allegory).

Writing

  • Writing analytical essays focused on literary analysis, with well-supported arguments and evidence.
  • Writing persuasive essays, research papers, and personal reflections.
  • Using a clear and formal writing style, incorporating appropriate tone and diction.

Grammar

  • Mastering the use of subordinate and relative clauses in complex and compound-complex sentences.
  • Correctly using punctuation, such as semicolons, colons, and quotation marks, to improve sentence clarity.
  • Using modals and conditionals effectively in different contexts.

Literary Elements

  • Understanding the significance of point of view, setting, and character development in various texts.
  • Exploring the impact of the historical and cultural context on the writing and themes of a text.
  • Comparing different literary movements (e.g., Romanticism, Realism, Modernism) and their influence on writing style.

Grade 10 English & Literature

Reading Comprehension

  • Analyzing and evaluating themes, character development, and setting in complex literary texts.
  • Identifying narrative techniques such as stream of consciousness, unreliable narrators, and non-linear timelines.
  • Engaging critically with non-fiction texts, understanding the author's argument, and identifying rhetorical strategies.

Vocabulary Development

  • Mastering academic vocabulary used in formal and informal discourse.
  • Understanding and using advanced rhetorical devices in writing and speech (e.g., ethos, pathos, logos).
  • Building a diverse vocabulary for literary analysis and argumentation.

Writing

  • Writing literary analysis essays that explore thematic elements, character relationships, and narrative techniques.
  • Writing persuasive essays with logical, coherent arguments and well-organized paragraphs.
  • Developing and refining writing skills for various types of essays (descriptive, narrative, expository, argumentative).

Grammar

  • Using advanced grammatical structures to express complex ideas effectively (e.g., inversion, parallelism).
  • Applying advanced punctuation rules to enhance clarity and style.
  • Identifying and correcting errors in sentence structure, tense, and voice in their own writing.

Literary Elements

  • Understanding and analyzing the role of symbols, motifs, and allegories in shaping the theme of a text.
  • Exploring literary movements and genres (e.g., Naturalism, Modernism) and their impact on literature.
  • Critically engaging with the text's historical, cultural, and social contexts.
