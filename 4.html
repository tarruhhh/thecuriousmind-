Grade 4 Mathematics Curriculum

Mathematics Concepts for Grade 4

In Grade 4, students will build upon the foundation they developed in earlier grades and tackle more complex concepts in mathematics. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key topics that Grade 4 students will learn in math:

1. Advanced Multiplication and Division

Grade 4 students deepen their understanding of multiplication and division:

  • Multiply larger numbers (up to 4-digit numbers) by 1-digit and 2-digit numbers.
  • Divide larger numbers (up to 4-digit numbers) by 1-digit divisors, including with remainders.
  • Learn to use the distributive property to break down multiplication problems.
  • Solve word problems involving multiplication and division in real-world contexts.
  • Explore multiplication of multi-digit numbers by two-digit numbers.

2. Fractions and Decimals

Students in Grade 4 learn to work with fractions and decimals:

  • Compare and order fractions and decimals.
  • Learn to add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators by finding common denominators.
  • Convert between fractions and decimals (e.g., 1/2 = 0.5, 3/4 = 0.75).
  • Multiply and divide fractions by whole numbers (e.g., 2 Ã— 3/4 = 6/4).
  • Understand decimal place value (tenths, hundredths, thousandths) and represent decimals up to two decimal places.
  • Compare decimals and round decimals to the nearest tenth or hundredth.

3. Addition and Subtraction of Larger Numbers

In Grade 4, students work with larger numbers for addition and subtraction:

  • Add and subtract numbers up to 10,000 using regrouping (carrying and borrowing).
  • Solve multi-step word problems that involve addition and subtraction of large numbers.
  • Apply estimation techniques to check the reasonableness of sums and differences.
  • Use mental math strategies to add and subtract efficiently.

4. Measurement

Grade 4 students learn about both metric and customary units of measurement:

  • Convert between units of measurement (e.g., inches to feet, milliliters to liters, grams to kilograms).
  • Calculate the perimeter and area of regular and irregular shapes.
  • Measure and convert units of time, including hours, minutes, and seconds.
  • Use both standard (inches, feet, pounds) and metric (millimeters, centimeters, kilograms) systems of measurement.
  • Solve real-life problems involving the measurement of length, weight, and capacity.

5. Geometry and Angles

Grade 4 students deepen their understanding of geometry:

  • Classify and describe 2D shapes (e.g., rectangles, triangles, circles, polygons) and 3D shapes (e.g., cubes, cones, pyramids, spheres).
  • Identify and measure angles (right angles, acute angles, obtuse angles) using a protractor.
  • Learn about symmetry and identify lines of symmetry in shapes.
  • Understand the properties of different types of triangles (equilateral, isosceles, scalene).
  • Calculate the perimeter and area of simple polygons.

6. Data and Graphing

Students work with data and learn to represent it visually:

  • Collect and organize data in tables and lists.
  • Interpret data from bar graphs, pictographs, and line plots.
  • Analyze and solve problems using data in graphs.
  • Calculate the mean, median, and mode of a set of data.
  • Learn how to use different types of graphs to represent and interpret data clearly.

7. Word Problems and Real-Life Applications

Grade 4 students apply math to solve real-life problems:

  • Solve complex word problems that involve addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fractions, and decimals.
  • Use math to solve practical problems in everyday life, such as shopping, budgeting, and planning events.
  • Practice problem-solving strategies such as drawing diagrams, making lists, and breaking down complex problems into simpler steps.
  • Develop logical reasoning and critical thinking through math problems.
