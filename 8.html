Grade 8 Mathematics Curriculum

Mathematics Concepts for Grade 8

Grade 8 students build on their prior knowledge of algebra, geometry, and data analysis, diving deeper into algebraic expressions, linear equations, functions, and more advanced geometry. They also work with irrational numbers, more complex equations, and 2D and 3D geometry concepts.

1. Real Numbers and Their Operations

Grade 8 students deepen their understanding of real numbers:

  • Work with positive and negative numbers, including whole numbers, fractions, decimals, and irrational numbers (e.g., √2, π).
  • Understand the properties of real numbers and perform operations on them (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).
  • Apply the order of operations (PEMDAS) to solve complex expressions.
  • Identify and classify rational and irrational numbers.

2. Algebraic Expressions and Equations

Grade 8 students advance in algebra:

  • Simplify and evaluate algebraic expressions.
  • Expand and factor binomials (e.g., using the distributive property).
  • Solve multi-step equations involving variables (e.g., 2x + 5 = 3x - 7).
  • Work with linear equations, inequalities, and systems of equations.
  • Graph solutions to linear equations and inequalities on the coordinate plane.

3. Functions and Graphs

Grade 8 students explore functions and graphing:

  • Understand the concept of a function as a relationship between input and output.
  • Represent functions as equations, tables, and graphs (e.g., y = mx + b, the slope-intercept form).
  • Graph linear functions and interpret the slope and y-intercept in real-world contexts.
  • Understand and plot proportional relationships and graph horizontal and vertical lines.

4. Systems of Linear Equations

Grade 8 students solve systems of equations:

  • Understand and solve systems of linear equations using substitution and elimination methods.
  • Graph systems of linear equations and interpret the point of intersection as the solution.
  • Apply systems of equations to real-world problems, such as finding the point of intersection in business, economics, or engineering scenarios.

5. Geometry: Transformations and Congruence

Grade 8 students study transformations and congruence:

  • Understand and apply transformations, including translations, rotations, reflections, and dilations, on the coordinate plane.
  • Identify and classify congruent and similar figures.
  • Use symmetry, congruence, and transformations to solve problems related to geometric shapes and figures.
  • Understand and apply properties of reflections, rotations, and translations to real-world geometric designs.

6. Pythagorean Theorem

Grade 8 students apply the Pythagorean Theorem:

  • Use the Pythagorean Theorem to find missing side lengths in right-angled triangles (a² + b² = c²).
  • Apply the Pythagorean Theorem in real-life situations, such as construction and navigation.
  • Understand and use the converse of the Pythagorean Theorem to determine if a triangle is a right triangle.

7. Data Analysis and Probability

Grade 8 students analyze data and probability:

  • Organize and interpret data using stem-and-leaf plots, histograms, and box plots.
  • Calculate measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and dispersion (range, interquartile range).
  • Calculate and interpret probabilities of simple and compound events.
  • Use probability to make predictions and solve real-world problems involving chance.

8. Rational and Irrational Numbers

Grade 8 students work with rational and irrational numbers:

  • Identify and differentiate between rational and irrational numbers.
  • Simplify expressions involving rational numbers and work with their properties.
  • Approximate irrational numbers, such as π and square roots, using decimal representations.
  • Apply rational and irrational numbers to solve real-world problems involving measurements, rates, and scales.

9. Volume and Surface Area of 3D Shapes

Grade 8 students calculate the volume and surface area of 3D shapes:

  • Calculate the surface area and volume of cones, spheres, cylinders, and pyramids.
  • Apply formulas to find the surface area and volume of composite shapes (e.g., a cone on top of a cylinder).
  • Solve real-world problems involving the volume and surface area of 3D objects, such as packaging or construction.
